Grammar & Style

This page explains common grammar errors and gives tips to improve clarity and accuracy in writing.

Common Grammar Errors

  • Subject-Verb Agreement: "She run fast" ❌ → "She runs fast" ✅
  • Run-on Sentences: Too many ideas in one sentence without proper punctuation.
  • Sentence Fragments: Incomplete sentences, e.g., "Because I was tired." → should be joined with a main clause.
  • Misused Apostrophes: Confusing its (possessive) and it's (it is).
  • Confusing Words: Mixing up their/there/they‘re or your/you’re.

Learning Tips

  • Check Verb Tense: Make sure verbs match the time of the action.
  • Use Parallel Structure: Keep grammar patterns consistent in a list (e.g., "reading, writing, and drawing").
  • Modifiers: Place adjectives/adverbs near the words they describe to avoid confusion.
  • Pronoun Clarity: Ensure pronouns clearly refer to the correct noun.
  • Punctuation Practice: Use commas, periods, and quotation marks correctly.
